Databases & Websites

A library database is an online resource (not a website) that we subscribe to/pay for. That contains articles and information from print sources such as magazines, newspapers, journals and reference books. An article database enables you to search through thousands, sometimes millions, of different magazines, journals and newspapers to find articles on a particular topic. Some of the articles you will find are Full Text.

Read our magazines from your mobile device:

  1. Download the Flipster app from your mobile device's app store.
  2. Select your school "WORCESTER TECHNICAL HIGH SCHOOL".
  3. Enter our EBSCO user id and password.
  4. These instructions only need to be done the first time. After that opening the app will bring you to the magazines.

MAGAZINES NOW AVAILABLE ON SORA!

Finding the Titles

  • You should see the collection called "Always Available Magazines" as the second curation on the Sora Explore page. If you’re NOT seeing this, it means that you and/or someone has done some custom curations on your page. You can either revert your page back to the shared collection OR create your own curation of the magazines.
  • Regardless of custom curation status, you can now click on the "Magazines" button toward the top left-hand side of the page to view all titles.
  • You can also use Sora Filters > Format > Magazines to view the titles.

Borrowing the Tiles

  • Borrow a magazine title like you would any other title in Sora. Click the "Select Another Issue" button to see what other issues are available.
  • All magazine titles have the "always available" label which means that they’re available in unlimited simultaneous use for everyone and there are no limits on the number of individuals that can have an issue checked out at any time.
  • Unlike shared collection eBook and audiobooks, magazines do NOT count toward the total number of items that can be checked out at a time (5). It is possible to have 5 eBooks/audiobooks checked out and then a large number of magazine checkouts simultaneously.
  • Because magazine titles have the "always available" label, they are able to be assigned to students, if desired.

Reading the Titles

  • There are about 284 unique titles in the magazine collection with titles in English, Spanish, French, Portuguese, and a few others. I have attached a current list of titles. I will also be updating the standing curation as new titles are added.
  • If there are any new titles to add, they are added quarterly. I believe new issues are loaded according to the interval of the individual title.
